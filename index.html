What Causes Hair Loss?

Thinning or shedding of strands may occur due to various underlying reasons. Recognizing these factors is an important first step in determining the most appropriate action.

1. Genetic Factors

Androgenetic alopecia, often referred to as male or female pattern baldness, is a widespread condition that leads to gradual hair thinning over time. This condition is inherited, meaning it runs in families and is passed down through genes. It typically develops slowly over time, starting in early adulthood and becoming more noticeable as the years go by.

2. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal imbalances caused by pregnancy and menopause can significantly affect the hair growth cycle, often leading to noticeable thinning. Additionally, thyroid disorders and other medical conditions may disrupt normal hormone levels, leading to noticeable changes in hair growth and thickness, either temporarily or over an extended period. Understanding these underlying causes is important for choosing the right treatment approach.

3. Medical Conditions and Medications

Certain illnesses, such as alopecia areata, scalp infections, and autoimmune diseases, can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and result in visible thinning or shedding. These conditions often cause inflammation or damage to hair follicles, making it difficult for new hair to grow. Additionally, various medications prescribed for cancer, arthritis, depression, and heart problems may have side effects that include thinning hair or increased shedding.

4. Nutritional Deficiencies

Hair health depends greatly on the availability of key nutrients, and deficiencies can weaken the structure and function of hair follicles. Essential nutrients like iron, protein, and vitamins such as B12 and D are critical in maintaining hair strength and growth. Hair can become brittle, thin, and prone to shedding when these nutrients are lacking due to poor diet or absorption issues.

5. Physical and Emotional Stress

Experiencing severe physical trauma, surgery, or emotional stress can disrupt the body’s normal hair cycle, causing a condition called telogen effluvium. This leads to a premature shift of hair follicles into the resting phase, resulting in increased hair shedding weeks or months after the stressful event. While often temporary, this condition can be distressing and requires time and care to recover.

6. Lifestyle Factors

Frequent harsh chemical treatments, such as bleaching or perming, can weaken hair strands and contribute to breakage and thinning. Frequent use of heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons can damage the hair cuticle, often resulting in dryness and breakage. Poor hair care practices, including infrequent washing or rough handling, may exacerbate damage and negatively affect hair density.

Signs That May Indicate a Change in Hair Health

Noticing changes in your hair early can make a meaningful difference in addressing concerns effectively. Some signs to watch for include:

  • Noticeable thinning on the scalp or crown
  • Gradual widening of the part
  • Excessive shedding during washing or brushing
  • Bald patches or uneven hair distribution

If any signs appear, consulting a professional can help determine the cause and appropriate steps.

Customized Treatment Plans

Treatment may combine multiple approaches depending on individual needs. Common options include:

  • Medical Therapies: To support scalp health and encourage natural regrowth, medications such as topical minoxidil or certain prescriptions may be recommended.
  • Nutritional Support: Dietary advice and supplements are often part of the program to ensure the scalp and hair follicles receive necessary nourishment.
  • Laser Therapy: Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) is a noninvasive option for stimulating hair follicles, improving circulation, and supporting healthy hair growth.
  • Myofascial Release Therapy: This specialized therapy helps relieve tension and improve blood flow to the scalp. This therapeutic technique targets fascia tightness that can contribute to poor circulation around hair follicles, supporting a healthier environment for hair growth.
  • Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy: PRP therapy uses the patient’s blood plasma, rich in growth factors, injected into the scalp to stimulate hair follicles and encourage regrowth.
